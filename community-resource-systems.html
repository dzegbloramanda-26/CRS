Community Resource Systems (CRS) is a Ghanaian social development organisation working at the intersection of community knowledge, institutional capacity, and systemic change. We support communities and organisations to turn evidence-based ideas into practical, impactful action.

Our flexible approach means we tailor our support to the unique needs of our partners — whether it is co-designing programmes, building leadership, measuring impact, or facilitating community-led change. We believe lasting development is built from within, not delivered from outside.

What We Do

Services built for
Ghanaian realities.

Our services are not off-the-shelf. Every engagement is shaped by context, driven by community knowledge, and measured by outcomes that matter to the people we serve across Ghana.

01

Research & Evaluation

We design rigorous, contextually sensitive research that generates evidence speaking to Ghanaian realities — informing decision-making at community, district, and national levels. We specialise in participatory, mixed-method approaches that integrate community knowledge with quantitative evidence.

02

Strategy & Leadership

We support NGOs, government bodies, and CBOs across Ghana to develop strategies grounded in local context and accountable to those they serve. We facilitate strategic planning, organisational review, and leadership development for institutions at every level.

03

Capability Strengthening

From District Assemblies to national institutions, we build the skills, knowledge, and institutional capacity to sustain impact long after our engagement ends. Training, mentoring, and embedded advisory support delivered with a commitment to local ownership.

04

Community Facilitation

Participatory methods refined across Ghana's diverse communities — creating spaces where people drive their own development as architects, not beneficiaries, of change. We facilitate community planning, dialogue, visioning, and conflict transformation processes.

05

Livelihoods & Inclusion

We design and evaluate livelihood programmes addressing structural inequity — with focus on women, youth, persons with disabilities, and marginalised populations across Ghana. Market systems development, enterprise support, and financial inclusion strategies.

06

Policy & Governance

We work with Ghana's ministries, development partners, and district authorities to translate evidence into policy — building governance systems that are responsive, equitable, and durable. Policy analysis, reform facilitation, and stakeholder engagement.

How We Deliver

Tailored to context.
Not a template.

We do not offer pre-packaged programmes. Each engagement begins with listening — to your community, your data, your constraints. We then co-design the right combination of services for your specific goals.

  • Organisational assessments and needs analysis tailored to your institutional context
  • Co-designed monitoring, evaluation, and learning (MEL) frameworks
  • Long-term embedded support — not short-term consultancy visits
  • Training and mentoring for local staff and community facilitators
  • Policy briefs, reports, and communications for diverse audiences
  • Partnership brokering with government bodies, NGOs, and funders

How We Work

Locally rooted.
Evidence-led.

Our approach is not a formula. It is a set of deeply held commitments about how change happens — and who leads it. Lasting development is built from within communities, not delivered to them.

Our Four Phases

The CRS Method
I
Entry & Orientation

Begin With Community

Every engagement starts with deep listening. We enter with humility — not with a predetermined framework, but with genuine commitment to understanding what is already present: the strengths, networks, tensions, and aspirations that exist long before we arrive.

This means time on the ground. Conversations with elders, youth, women's groups, market traders, teachers, and local officials. We do not begin designing until we understand.

Stakeholder MappingCommunity ListeningContextual AnalysisPower Assessment
II
Design & Planning

Co-design With Partners

We work in genuine partnership — with community members, traditional authorities, local government, and civil society — to develop approaches that reflect Ghanaian knowledge systems and institutional realities.

Co-design is not a workshop. It is an ongoing, iterative process of dialogue, testing ideas, revising plans, and building shared ownership of what comes next.

Participatory DesignTheory of ChangeMEL FrameworkRisk Mapping
III
Implementation & Adaptation

Act, Learn, Adjust

Ghana's social and political contexts are dynamic. We build adaptive monitoring and real-time learning into every programme — not as add-ons, but as central to how we work.

Regular sense-making sessions with partners, rapid feedback loops with communities, and the institutional courage to change course when evidence demands it.

Adaptive ManagementReal-time DataCommunity FeedbackIterative Evaluation
IV
Exit & Sustainability

Leave Lasting Capacity

Our measure of success is not what we deliver — it is what communities and institutions can sustain, lead, and grow long after our direct engagement ends.

We build for permanence, not dependency. Every engagement includes a deliberate transition plan developed with — not for — our partners.

Transition PlanningSkills TransferSystems EmbeddingFinal Evaluation

Approach — Values

What we
stand for.

Our values are not aspirational statements. They are the commitments that shape every decision we make — who we partner with, how we engage, and what we are willing to say out loud.

Our Six Commitments

Ɔ

Community First

Communities are not the objects of development — they are its authors. Every decision we make is measured against: does this serve the people most directly affected?

◎

Evidence & Honesty

We follow the evidence, even when inconvenient. We tell our partners what we find, not what they want to hear. Rigour without honesty is performance — not development.

⊕

Genuine Partnership

We share risk, learn together, and remain accountable to the communities our work touches. We are partners who stay — not consultants who visit.

△

Equity & Inclusion

We actively work to surface and address power imbalances — within communities, within organisations, and within the systems we seek to change.

∞

Sustainability

We build for the long term. Every programme is designed with exit in mind — transferring skills, embedding systems, and strengthening local structures that outlast us.

◇

Contextual Humility

Ghana is not a monolith. What works in one community may not work in another. We approach every context with curiosity and the willingness to unlearn and begin again.

Our Work

Work that speaks
for itself.

Across Ghana — from coastal fishing communities to urban centres to smallholder farms in the north — our projects are as varied as the communities they serve.

Smallholder Farmer Inclusion Programme

◎ Upper East & Upper West Regions

2021–2024Health Systems

Community Health Systems Strengthening

◎ Ashanti & Bono Regions

Building capacity of community health workers, CHPS compounds, and district health teams to deliver integrated primary care with community oversight mechanisms.

2023–2026Youth

Youth Leadership & Civic Agency

◎ Greater Accra Region

Supporting young people in urban areas to develop civic skills, engage local government, and build peer-led advocacy networks in their own communities.

2020–2023Governance

District Assembly Capacity Building

◎ Central & Western Regions

Supporting District Assemblies to improve participatory planning, citizen engagement, and revenue mobilisation — strengthening Ghana's decentralisation system.

2022–2025Gender Equity

Women's Economic Networks

◎ Volta & Oti Regions

Establishing and strengthening women-led savings and enterprise groups, linking them to formal financial services and building sustainable peer support structures.

2023–2026Environment

Climate-Resilient Community Planning

◎ Northern Region

Integrating climate risk into community development plans in the northern savanna — building local adaptation strategies grounded in indigenous environmental knowledge.
